The Problem We Saw

Schools teach you what to think.
Almost never how to say it.

"The students who thrived weren't always the smartest in the room. They were almost always the ones who could communicate."

Having gone through this school district ourselves, we watched it happen year after year: students with sharp minds and real ideas who shrank the moment it was time to say them out loud. A book report here, a class presentation there — and that was it. Nobody was being taught to actually speak.

We also saw the other side: students who weren't necessarily the top of their class, but who could stand up, make their case, and hold a room's attention. Those students won competitions, led groups, and were heard. The difference was never intelligence — it was communication.

CLEAR Academy exists to close that gap. Starting in grades 3–5, before the habit of staying quiet becomes permanent. We teach public speaking, argument structure, and respectful debate — the skills that matter in every room your child will ever walk into.

01

Almost no public speaking instruction before middle school.

A few class presentations per year isn't training. Without deliberate instruction, students who are already nervous stay nervous — and the confidence gap widens every year.

02

Communication is the most transferable skill your child can develop.

From class discussions to job interviews to advocating for themselves in any situation — people who can organize their thoughts and deliver them clearly win more often, in more contexts.

03

Debate doesn't just teach you to talk. It teaches you to think.

Structured debate forces students to listen carefully, evaluate evidence, and build logical arguments. These habits of mind outlast any trophy or certificate.

How the Program Works

Structured for real attention. Built to actually work.

CLEAR Academy addresses what most school programs get wrong: too many students, too little feedback, too much passivity. Our structure is the opposite of that.

👥

Small Classes

We keep our classes small — deliberately. Every student gets real, specific feedback after every speech and every exercise. Public speaking only improves when you practice frequently and hear exactly what to fix. Small classes make both happen every session.

🔬

Within-Class Labs

Every session includes smaller lab breakouts inside the class. Students work in skill-level groups so everyone is challenged at the right level — whether they're speaking in front of others for the first time or already building arguments. No one is held back. No one is left behind.

📈

Fluid, Progress-Paced Curriculum

There are no rigid weekly locksteps. We move through phases when students are ready — not on a calendar. Grades 3, 4, and 5 all learn the same curriculum together; the lab structure handles differentiation so the whole class benefits from every perspective in the room.

🎯

Technical Feedback Every Session

After every speech and debate round, students receive specific, structured feedback — not just "good job." Instructors use consistent language tied to our rubric so every student always knows exactly what they did well and what to work on next.

The Curriculum

Six phases. One clear path.

Every CLEAR Academy student moves through the same six phases — from the first time they stand up to speak, all the way to competing in a structured tournament. Each phase builds directly on the last. You can't build arguments before you can speak clearly. You can't debate before you can argue.

1
Phase One
Finding Your Voice
What We Teach
  • The four pillars of delivery: volume, pace, eye contact, and posture
  • How to project your voice and fill a room with presence
  • Controlling your pace — owning silence instead of rushing through nerves
  • Understanding and managing stage fright: why it happens and how to use it
  • Short unscripted speeches in front of the group, with structured positive feedback
Skills Students Build
🎤 Voice projection 👁 Eye contact 🐢 Controlled pace 🧍 Stage presence 😤 Managing nerves 💪 Confidence in front of others
2
Phase Two
Shaping Your Ideas
What We Teach
  • The difference between rambling and purposeful communication
  • How to organize thoughts before speaking: clear beginning, middle, and end
  • Speaking with a destination — every statement should go somewhere
  • Structured short speeches with a defined topic, supporting points, and conclusion
  • Timed delivery and developing self-awareness about length and clarity
Skills Students Build
🗂 Thought organization 🎯 Speaking with purpose 📖 Structured delivery ✂️ Clarity & concision ⏱ Time awareness
3
Phase Three
Building Your Argument
What We Teach
  • The difference between an opinion and a supported argument
  • The P.E.E. framework: Point → Evidence → Explanation
  • What makes strong evidence vs. weak evidence
  • Basic source evaluation: why some information is more credible than others
  • Constructing and delivering a full argument on an assigned topic
Skills Students Build
🧱 Argument structure 🔍 Evidence evaluation 📰 Source credibility ⚖️ Opinion vs. argument 🎯 Logical reasoning 💬 Persuasive speaking
4
Phase Four
Listening & Responding
What We Teach
  • Active listening: how to hear what someone is actually saying, not just waiting for your turn
  • The RREA rebuttal framework: Repeat → Refute → Evidence → Alternative
  • How to paraphrase an opponent's argument accurately before countering it
  • Identifying the weak point in an argument and targeting it precisely
  • Rapid-fire rebuttal drills under time pressure
Skills Students Build
👂 Active listening 🔁 Accurate paraphrasing ⚡ Quick thinking 🧩 Rebuttal structure (RREA) 🤝 Respectful disagreement
5
Phase Five
Entering the Debate
What We Teach
  • Formal debate format: timed speeches, constructive arguments, cross-examination, and rebuttals
  • How to prepare a full case for an assigned position — including one you disagree with
  • The same format, vocabulary, and structure used in high school competitive debate
  • Full structured debate rounds with technical, written feedback after every round
  • Self-evaluation: how to assess your own performance and set improvement goals
Skills Students Build
🎙 Formal debate format 📋 Case preparation 🎭 Arguing any position 🔎 Cross-examination 📊 Technical self-evaluation 🏛 Competitive debate vocab
6
Phase Six
The Tournament
What We Teach
  • Multiple rounds of structured debate in a real competitive format
  • Topic revealed in advance, sides assigned, timed speeches, written judge feedback each round
  • Performance under genuine competitive pressure — a first for most students
  • How to learn from watching other rounds as an informed, critical audience member
  • Completing a full competitive program: every participant receives a certificate
What Students Take Away
🏆 Tournament experience 🔥 Performance under pressure 📝 Written feedback 🎓 Certificate of completion 🚀 Head start for HS debate 💬 A voice they earned

The Tournament

Competitive format. Real stakes. Built to mean something.

The CLEAR Academy Mini Tournament is a fully structured competitive event modeled after the format used in high school debate nationally — because the goal has always been to prepare students for what's ahead, not just celebrate what they've done. Every student competes, receives written feedback, and leaves with a certificate.

R1

Round 1 — Open Draw

All students compete. Pairings assigned. Topic revealed with prep time. Every student's first real competitive debate round.

R2

Round 2 — Adjusted Pairings

Reshuffled based on Round 1. Students switch sides — testing their command of both positions on the topic.

R3

Round 3 — New Topic

Fresh topic, limited prep time. Highest pressure round. Top performers advance to the final.

🏆

Finals + Awards

Top pairs debate in front of the full audience. Every student receives written feedback and a certificate of completion.

Questions from Parents

The things families ask us most.

We'd rather answer too many questions than leave any parent uncertain about what their child is walking into.

My child is shy. Is this right for them?

Especially so. Every shy student who's walked through this program has grown the most. Phase 1 exists entirely to build the floor before anything else is introduced. We never throw students into the deep end.

Do students need any prior experience?

None at all. We begin at zero — how to stand, how to project, how to make eye contact. No experience is assumed or required.

How are different grade levels handled?

All grades move through the same curriculum together. Within each session, lab breakouts allow students to work at their level — so no one is overwhelmed and advanced students are always challenged.

Why is there no strict weekly schedule?

Because rigid pacing serves the teacher, not the student. We move when students are ready to move. Some cohorts fly through phases; others need more time. Both outcomes are completely fine.

Is the tournament a lot of pressure?

The tournament has competitive stakes. The program leading to it doesn't. Students are only ever compared to their own previous performance — not to each other — until the tournament itself.

What does "modeled on HS debate" mean for a 4th grader?

Same format and vocabulary, age-appropriate topics. Students who join high school debate later will walk in already knowing the structure — a real head start over their peers.
